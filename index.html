Dreams dominate the human experience; They let us escape beyond the daily hustle, with its regrets and bitterness, to something sweeter — something too good to be true. They are more precious than reality: they satisfy the cravings that aren't fulfilled, replacing them with hope.
But what happens when dreams fail?
What defines success in one's dreams?
Do we all need a common dream?

A dream is a “cherished ambition, aspiration, or ideal.” Ironically, dreams are what we encounter in our sleep. We know it's not real. It's a fantasy, but a sweet one. Yet in the context of the human existence, we use this word “dream” to describe aspirations that we greatly desire. we desire to pursue dreams even though Even though dreams are held by the individual, many dreams are collective and shared by a community. The American dream reflects the collective dream of many Americans and is very harmful to many individuals within American society.
